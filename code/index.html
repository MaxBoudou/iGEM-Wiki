Handling Antibiotic
resistance with care !

Infections caused by antibiotic-resistant germs are difficult, and sometimes impossible, to treat. In most cases, antibiotic-resistant infections require extended hospital stays, additional follow-up doctor visits, and costly and toxic alternatives.

No new family of antibiotics has been commercialized. Today antibiotics are considered as one of the biggest therapeutic advances of the 20th century.

10 million deaths could occur annually by 2050, according to the World Health Organization, if effective intervention methods against antimicrobial resistance are not made.

In hospitals, this resistance results in a rise of nosocomial infections. This is caused by the lack of adapted medication to specific germs which are particularly resistants, such as the Escherichia Coli. According to the French Health Department, it's estimated that hospital-acquired infections are the cause of 4000 deaths per year in France.

That's why we, the IGEM IONIS 2020 Team, are actively battling against this matter. We're looking to find an alternative to antibiotics via synthetic biology. To go further that just understanding the living, we wish to "redesign" it, by associating biology and digitial technologies. We founded the BacTail project, whose goal is to use some of the bacteriophages' genes, giving them properties such as recognition or bacterial lysis. A bacteriophage is in fact a virus, which only infects bacterias.
